## Is This Too Good to Be True?

**Common Objections & Honest Answers:**

**"Will students actually use this?"**  
Students participate when it's easy, private, and rewarding. EchoDeed takes 30 seconds to share a kindness act (vs. 5-10 minutes for written reflections). Anonymous posting removes social pressure. Rewards create positive reinforcement.

**"What if we only get a handful of posts?"**  
Even 50 documented acts per month is 600/year - far more evidence than most schools have. And once students see peers participating, engagement grows organically.

**"Don't we still need to teach character education lessons?"**  
Absolutely. EchoDeed doesn't replace curriculum - it complements it by capturing evidence of practice. Teach empathy in class, then students demonstrate it in real life and EchoDeed documents it.

**"What about schools without 1:1 devices?"**  
Students can access EchoDeed on any device (school computers, library, home). Even 2-3 logins per week per student generates substantial documentation.
