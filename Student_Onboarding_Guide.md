## üí¨ **Student FAQ (Real Questions from Real Students)**

### **Q: "Isn't this just fake positivity?"**
**A:** Nope! We're not asking you to pretend everything's perfect. This is about noticing the good stuff that actually happens every day. Real kindness, real people, real impact.

### **Q: "Will people think I'm weird for posting nice things?"**
**A:** Remember, it's anonymous! No one knows who's posting what. Plus, most people secretly appreciate positivity - they're just afraid to show it.

### **Q: "What if I don't see kindness happening?"**
**A:** Start small. Someone held a door, someone said thanks, someone smiled. Once you start looking for it, you'll see it everywhere. Or be the kindness you want to see!

### **Q: "This seems like extra work. Why should I bother?"**
**A:** It literally takes 30 seconds to post something. And reading the feed during boring moments is way more uplifting than doom-scrolling social media.

### **Q: "What if my friends make fun of me?"**
**A:** Real friends support things that make you happy and help others. If someone makes fun of kindness, that says more about them than about you.

### **Q: "Can teachers see what I post?"**
**A:** They can see the posts, but they can't tell who wrote them. Your posts are anonymous even to school staff.

### **Q: "What if I accidentally identify someone?"**
**A:** Keep descriptions general. Instead of "Sarah helped me with math," write "Someone helped me understand a difficult math problem." Focus on the act, not the person.

### **Q: "Do I have to participate?"**
**A:** Absolutely not! You can just read others' posts, or not use it at all. There's zero pressure to post anything.
