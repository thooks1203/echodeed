# EchoDeed™ Market Research Brief
*$47B Total Addressable Market Analysis*

## Executive Summary
EchoDeed operates in the intersection of two large, growing markets: K-8 Social-Emotional Learning ($655M) and Corporate Wellness ($46.4B). Our dual-market approach uses education for low-cost customer validation, then scales into high-margin corporate wellness revenue.

**Key Market Insights:**
- **Post-pandemic urgency:** 70% of teachers report worsened student behavior
- **Measurement gap:** Current SEL programs lack quantifiable outcomes

- **Anonymous feedback void:** Existing tools require identification, limiting authenticity

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Education Validation (Months 1-12)
**Objective:** Prove product-market fit with quantifiable outcomes

- **Target:** 20 K-8 schools for pilot programs
- **Pricing:** $3,000-5,000 annual contracts
- **Sales approach:** Conference presence, administrator networks
- **Success metrics:** >70% student engagement, >80% administrator satisfaction

### Phase 2: Corporate Expansion (Months 13-24)
**Objective:** Scale into high-margin corporate wellness market

- **Target:** 8-12 mid-market companies (200-1,000 employees)
- **Pricing:** $8-15 per employee per month
- **Sales approach:** Enterprise sales with education references
- **Success metrics:** $50K+ average contract value, <8% annual churn

### Phase 3: Enterprise Scale (Months 25-36)
**Objective:** Large enterprise accounts and market leadership

- **Target:** 5-10 enterprise accounts (1,000+ employees)
- **Pricing:** $100K-500K annual contracts
- **Sales approach:** Dedicated enterprise sales team
- **Success metrics:** $200K+ average contract value, predictive accuracy >85%

## Market Risks & Mitigations

### Primary Risks:
1. **Market adoption slower than projected**
   - *Mitigation:* Working trial system enables real-time validation and adjustment
2. **Large competitors entering anonymous wellness space**
   - *Mitigation:* First-mover advantage and education market relationships
3. **Economic downturn reducing wellness budgets**
   - *Mitigation:* Dual market approach and conservative pricing

### Regulatory Considerations:
- **Education:** COPPA, FERPA compliance (frameworks implemented)
- **Corporate:** GDPR, state privacy laws (privacy-first architecture)
- **Healthcare:** Not medical device, wellness tool only
- **Labor:** Anonymous data avoids employee relations issues
